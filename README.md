A chocolate chip cookie is a cookie that has chocolate chips as its main ingredient which is cooked along with the cookie giving a chocolaty flavour to the plain cookie. Chocolate chip cookies originated in the United States around 1938, when Ruth Graves Wakefield chopped up a Nestl√© semi-sweet chocolate bar and added the chopped chocolate to a cookie recipe.

Ingredients:

1 cup butter, softened
1 cup white sugar
1 cup packed brown sugar
2 eggs
2 teaspoons vanilla extract
1 teaspoon baking soda
2 teaspoons hot water
1/2 teaspoon salt
3 cups all-purpose flour
2 cups chocolate chips



Recipe:

Step 1
Preheat oven to 350 degrees F (175 degrees C).

Step 2
Cream together the butter, white sugar, and brown sugar until smooth. Beat in the eggs one at a time, then stir in the vanilla. Dissolve baking soda in hot water. Add to batter along with salt. Stir in flour and chocolate chips. Drop by large spoonfuls onto ungreased pans.

Step 3
Bake for about 10 minutes in the preheated oven, or until edges are nicely browned.

Step 4
Serve in a bowl or plate and enjoy!
